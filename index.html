Family care coordination

When everyone who loves someone
is holding a different picture

When someone in a family needs care — a serious illness, a terminal diagnosis, a mental health crisis — the coordination burden falls on the people who love them. Different family members hold different information. Different assumptions about what is happening and what to do. No one is working from the same picture.

Sameground derives the shared picture. Not a medical care plan — that belongs to the clinicians. The coordination document that makes it possible for everyone who loves this person to act together, from the same ground.

You describe the situation. We produce a Care Coordination document — specific to your family, specific to this moment — and send it to you by email within minutes.

Your situation

Tell us what is happening

Answer as specifically as you can. The more detail you give, the more useful the response will be. There are no wrong answers — uncertainty is more useful than false confidence. Use first names or initials only.

Question 1

Who needs care? What is their condition? What does daily life look like right now for them and for the people caring for them?

Question 2

Name every family member who is part of this — present or absent, helping or not helping. What does each one currently understand? What is each one doing?

Question 3

What does one family member know that another doesn't? Where are people pulling in different directions? What is the thing nobody has said out loud yet?

Question 4

Have they been asked directly? What have they said? If they cannot speak for themselves — what do you know about what they would want, and how do you know it?

Question 5

Not what you think about it — what it feels like to carry this. Who is overwhelmed? What feels unsustainable?

Question 6

What is stopping the obvious next step? What has already been tried?
